Purpose

This Web portal is in service to my Final Project for DATA 608 (Knowledge & Visual Analytics), a core course in CUNY's Master of Science in Data Science program. You may access specific dashboards by clicking on the "Donors" and "Recipients" item in the top menu.

Context

The issues surrounding development aid is always pressing in the geo-political arena, perhaps now more than ever before. Its impacts (both positive and negative) affect both the recipient and donor countries, and they go beyond dollars and cents. These issues include accountability (by both donor and recipients), humanitarian, diplomatic, social, and even immigration issues, to name a few. Recently the United States of America announced cuts to the budget of its long standing USAID, the world’s leading international development agency. The debates on whether international aid really help these developing countries and whether countries should donate their own money for the benefits of the citizens of other countries could go on forever. Recently, President Donald Trump has threatened to cut foreign aid to Central American countries (Guatemala, Honduras and El Salvador) from which migrant caravans travel to the US to seek asylum. People react differently to these realities based on experiences, fear, and beliefs. How many of these reactions are based on facts/data? How many US and European citizens know just how much of their tax dollars goes towards aid in Central America and other developing countries? How many of the Central Americans and citizens of other developing countries actually know the magnitude of the aid given to them by other countries.

Obviously the concept of foreign aid is a very complex and delicate matter, but knowing these figures could empower the citizens of poor developing nations and the more prosperous donor nations to demand accountability on the part their leaders. Equipping the layperson with such information will improve their communications among themselves and with their leaders. It is in this light that I am proposing to build a dashboard where information on the disbursements of development aid across the globe can be easily accessible. Obviously, these data do not stand on their own. One must have an appreciation of history to understand the issues surrounding development aid and its possible ties to other issues such as international chain migration.

Uitility

The portal will allow users to answer the following questions:

Donors
• How much do we donate in a given year to a particular country?
• How does our contribution compare to that of other donating countries.
• What percentage of our GDP do we provide as aid?
• What is the distribution of our aid across the globe?
• How are our aid distributed across regions in the world?
• What types of aid do we provide and what does their distribution looks like?
• What are the top countries we provide aid?

Recipients
• How much aid do we receive from a specific country?
• How many countries provide aid to us?
• How much aid do we receive per capita?
• Historically what aid have we received?
• How does our aid compare to other countries?

Data

The funding data was sourced from the Organisation for Economic Cooperation and Development’s (OECD) iLibrary. Specifically, their Official Development Assistance (ODA) detailed aid statistics that provides comprehensive data on the volume, origin, and types of foreign aid and other resource flows from donor countries to recipient countries. The ODA data are provided by OECD members of the Development Assistance Committee (DAC). More information may be found on their website at https://www.oecd-ilibrary.org. A link to the data can be found at https://stats.oecd.org/viewhtml.aspx?datasetcode=TABLE2A&lang=en. The data is freely accessible to the public and is cited as specified on their website.

The GDP and Per Capita data will be sourced from the World Bank’s Open Data portal. This is also a publicly available data set and is cited as specified. More information on the terms and conditions of the usage of their data sets can be found here. The data set can be found here.
